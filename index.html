THECONVERSATION REDESIGN

Client:TheConversation Columbia University project

Project:UX/UI Redesign

Work: Creative Direction, UX/UI Design, Research

Time: December, 2022

TheConversation is a multimedia platform that helps life long learners find credible, trustworthy content created by academics. The Conversation combines academic rigor with journalistic flair. It is a destination to many curious learners worldwide for high-quality information about current affairs, science, art, and culture. Even though TheConversation is an excellent source of high quality content, we recognized some room for improvement in its design and content organization. The original platform seems to be very crowded, and it makes it hard to find articles that people may enjoy based on their interest. So that was our starting point in re-imagining TheConversation.

USER RESEARCH

We conducted 4 interviews to learn about ways in which people engage with multimedia platforms like TheConversation. We wanted to know mainly about the pain points they experience.

And we have learned that people struggle with

01 the credibility of information they engage with

02 limited time for learning

03 disorganized content they often encounter

04 mistrust of the expertise of the authors

05 lack of personally relevant format of learning and content

06 poor and distracting design

Our Qualtrics survey (n=18) has revealed a similar story. When engaging with the content focused on current affairs, science, art, and culture, our audience - life long learners – appreciate:

01 multisensory and personalised learning

02 content focused on their preferred way of learning

03 clear, well-designed and organised content

04 “escaping their bubbles” - that is learning

information outside of their primary interests

05 access to short form content

06 sharing what they learn with friends

07 the expertise of an author

USER PERSONA

Based on our affinity diagram and empathy map, we tried to re-imagine TheConversation for our user persona –Emilia Cole – who

01 looks for personally relevant and interesting content

02 likes to learn through multiple formats

03 feels overwhelmed with disorganized content online

04 hates being bombarded with ads

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Many curious learners worldwide want to consume high-quality information about science, current affairs, art, and culture, but they are often bombarded with distracting ads and disorganized, personally irrelevant, poorly designed, and lengthy content. How might we ensure our readers are confident in getting credible information, learning the way they prefer, and are able to share that information with others?

PRIORITIZATION MATRIX

Based on our research and testing we identified our top features:

01 personalization: picking personally relevant interests and

format of learning

02 multisensory learning: featuring content in many different sensory

modalities such as article, podcast, video, augmented reality, etc.

03 minimal design/clarity & organization – designing experiences that

are calming to the life long learner through minimal design,

and soothing, clean aesthetics

UI STYLE GUIDE

Based on our research and feedback from our testing, we created the style guide. We wanted the personality of our design to be friendly, nerdy, minimal, and clear.

FURTHER TESTING & ITERATIONS

Based on the feedback we obtained, we modified the font and added icons to several buttons and improved overall accessibility.

FUTURE DIRECTION

It would be fascinating to explore and develop further other features that were considered important for our life-long learners:

01 sharing content with family and friends

02 highlighting the expertise of and trust in the writers

03 highlighting the credibility of the content (fact checking)

04 building our “personalised library” feature further

05 and focusing on minimal design
